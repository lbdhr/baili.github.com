城市街道峡谷中植物隔离带对交通排放气态污染物扩散影响的研究
The impacts of roadside vegetation barriers on the dispersion of gaseous traffic pollution in urban street canyons

作者：李小兵

摘要： 机动车排放的污染物会对路侧行人的身心健康造成严重损害，因此在当今城市发展过程中已经有一系列的相关措施被应用于缓解交通给城市环境带来的大气污染问题。其中，城市道路的植物分隔带在进行机非分流和美化市容的同时对减少路侧行人的污染暴露水平也起到了重要的作用。在城市街道中，植物分隔带可以改变街道内交通排放污染物的扩散路径，进而改变交通排放污染物的浓度在城市街道中的空间分布形式，以到达改善城市街道中大气环境质量的目的。本论文以一氧化碳（CO）为示踪气并结合外场试验和计算流体动力学仿真（CFD）的方法，对机动车辆排放的气体污染物在不同类型街道中的扩散特性进行研究，并给出了植物分隔带在不同类型街道中的最优高度。研究结果表明，城市道路的植物分隔带可以明显的降低路侧行人对于机动车排放污染物的暴露水平。除此之外，当街道的宽深比（Aspect Ratio, 路侧建筑物的高度与街道宽度之比）在0.3到1.67之间时，1.1米可以作为植物隔离带的最优高度；当城市街道的宽深比小于0.3时，植物隔离带的最优高度可以在0.9米到2.5米之间选取。当植物分隔带处于最优高度时，路侧行人对于机动车辆排放的气体污染物的暴露水平最低。本研究的结论可以为城市道路植物分隔带的设计提供参考，同时对于降低城市道路两侧行人对交通排放污染物的暴露水平具有重要的实践意义。

Abstract: Many actions have been taken to alleviate traffic-induced air pollution in urban environment due to serious damages of traffic emissions to human health. The roadside vegetation barrier, which is originally used to isolate non-motorized traffics from motors, is one of the most effective methods to reduce the exposure level of pedestrians to traffic emissions. To attain the objective of improving air quality in urban street canyons, vegetation barriers always make it via changing dispersion patterns of traffic emissions over the slow lanes of streets. In this study we use field measurements and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) methods taking CO as a trace gas to describe diffusion characteristics of traffic emissions, and try to obtain the optimal vegetation barrier heights in different street types. Results indicate that roadside air quality at the pedestrian level is significantly improved with the presence of vegetation barriers. In addition, for street canyons (symmetrical) with an aspect ratio (the ratio of building height to street width) ranging from 0.3 to 1.67, 1.1 m can be used as an optimal height. For street canyons with an aspect ratio of lower than 0.3, 0.9 m to 2.5 m can be considered as the optimal heights for roadside vegetation barriers. With the optimal heights, the vegetation barriers are capable of reducing the exposure level of roadside pedestrians to the greatest extent. These findings are expected to provide important insights into alleviation of gaseous mobile emissions in terms of vegetation barrier design in urban streets.
